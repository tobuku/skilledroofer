Safety Gear

Critical Safety
3M / Guardian

Roofing Fall Protection Harness Kit

OSHA requires fall protection on roofs above 6 feet. A proper harness kit includes the harness, rope grab, lifeline, and roof anchor. Not optional — this saves lives.

  • Full body harness with adjustable straps
  • Rope grab and 50' lifeline
  • Reusable roof anchor bracket
  • OSHA/ANSI compliant

Cutting Tools

Milwaukee / Stanley

Hook Blade Utility Knife

A good utility knife with hook blades is used constantly on the roof — cutting shingles, trimming underlayment, opening bundles. Hook blades cut shingles cleanly without gouging the deck.

  • Hook blades designed for shingle cutting
  • Quick-change blade mechanism
  • Folding or retractable options
  • Blades are cheap — change them often
Around $15 – $25 Check Price →
Malco / Wiss

Metal Roofing Snips (Left, Right & Straight)

For metal roofing work, you need all three snips — left, right, and straight cut. Aviation-style snips from Malco or Wiss are the standard for cutting flashing, drip edge, and metal panels.

  • Set of 3: left, right, and straight cut
  • Cuts up to 18 gauge steel
  • Spring-loaded for less hand fatigue
  • Color-coded handles (red/green/yellow)

Measuring & Layout

Stanley / Irwin

Chalk Line Reel

You can't install straight courses without a chalk line. Snap lines every 5 inches (or whatever your shingle exposure is) to keep rows perfectly aligned across the roof.

  • 100' line for long runs
  • High-visibility blue or red chalk
  • Fast retract mechanism
  • Double-duty as a plumb bob
Around $10 – $15 Check Price →
Johnson Level

Pitch & Slope Finder

Know your roof pitch before you start estimating materials or setting up safety equipment. A pitch finder saves time vs. measuring rise over run manually.

  • Reads pitch instantly (X/12 format)

  • Compact — fits in your tool pouch
  • Essential for accurate material estimates

After every tear-off, roll a magnetic sweeper around the yard, driveway, and work area. One flat tire from a roofing nail costs more than this tool. Non-negotiable for professional crews.

  • 24" or 36" sweep width
  • Quick-release to dump collected nails
  • Adjustable handle height
  • Picks up nails through grass

Newborn / Cox

Heavy-Duty Caulk Gun

You'll go through cases of roofing sealant. A quality caulk gun with a high thrust ratio makes all-day caulking manageable. Get a dripless model — your pants will thank you.

  • 18:1 or 26:1 thrust ratio for thick sealants
  • Dripless mechanism
  • Comfortable grip for extended use
  • Works with standard 10 oz cartridges

💡

Pro Tip: Buy Quality Once

Cheap tools break mid-job. A quality roofing nailer, good snips, and proper safety gear pay for themselves in the first week. Buy the tool once, replace consumables (blades, pads, nails) as needed.
